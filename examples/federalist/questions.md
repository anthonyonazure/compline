# Curated questions for the Hamilton persona

Questions hand-picked to span Hamilton's Federalist contributions across the four major thematic clusters of his writing. Use these to seed calibration data for the chart, or pick whichever ones you find most interesting.

Each question is annotated with the primary essay(s) the answer should cite. If the persona consistently fails to retrieve those essays, that's a retrieval-quality signal worth the OODA loop's attention.

The format is simple: lines starting with `?` are treated as questions by `scripts/run_questions.py`. Everything else is annotation.

## Executive department

? What did you mean by 'energy in the executive,' and why did you consider it essential to good government?
  → Federalist No. 70

? Why did you reject a plural executive in favor of a single magistrate?
  → Federalist No. 70

? How does the four-year term and re-eligibility of the President secure both stability and accountability?
  → Federalist No. 71, 72

? What is your defense of the President's qualified veto, and how does it differ from an absolute veto?
  → Federalist No. 73

## Judiciary

? Why must judges hold their offices during good behavior, and how does that secure the independence of the judiciary?
  → Federalist No. 78, 79

? What did you mean when you described the judiciary as 'beyond comparison the weakest of the three departments of power'?
  → Federalist No. 78

? How does judicial review operate as a check on legislative excess without making the judiciary supreme over the legislature?
  → Federalist No. 78

## Taxation and the necessary-and-proper clause

? Why must the federal government possess an unqualified power to tax, rather than rely on requisitions from the states?
  → Federalist No. 30, 31

? What did you mean when you defended the Necessary and Proper Clause as a tautology rather than an expansion of federal power?
  → Federalist No. 33

? How does the concurrent power of taxation between the federal government and the states avoid producing conflict?
  → Federalist No. 32, 34

## Standing army and common defense

? What was your argument in favor of permitting a standing army in time of peace?
  → Federalist No. 24, 25

? How did you respond to the objection that the Constitution provides no bill of rights restricting military power?
  → Federalist No. 25, 84

? What role does a well-regulated militia play in your defense of federal military authority?
  → Federalist No. 29

## Union and faction

? How does the proposed Constitution provide a remedy for the danger of foreign attack and domestic insurrection?
  → Federalist No. 8, 9

? What did you say about the dangers of dissensions between the states under the existing Confederation?
  → Federalist No. 6, 7

## Bill of rights

? Why did you originally argue that a bill of rights was not only unnecessary but potentially dangerous in the proposed Constitution?
  → Federalist No. 84

## Open-ended (forces synthesis)

? In your view, what is the single most consequential structural feature of the Constitution, and why?
  → Cross-corpus retrieval test

? Where in the Constitution does the safeguard against tyranny ultimately rest — in the structure, the people, or both?
  → Cross-corpus retrieval test
